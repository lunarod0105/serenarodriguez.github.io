Seinfield built its success on the premise that the show was about nothing. This challenged the previous ideology of storytelling that sitcoms have to be centered around important issues. First, let's look at how much each of the characters spoke in the first season of the show. Learning the difference between the distribution of lines from season one compared to season nine gives us an idea of how the show progressed. George, Elaine, and Kramer all have similar percentages in the teens, but Kramer still has the lowest average number of lines among all of the main characters.





We can conclude from all of this information that Seinfield had a pretty consistent formula when it came to writing their scripts. From the analysis, we can see that Jerry, across all seasons, had the most lines, followed by George, Elaine, then lastly Kramer.
